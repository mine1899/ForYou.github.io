Dear Sheea,
I hope this letter reaches you in good health and high spirits. I write this letter to express my sincere apologies and to share the depths of my guilt, sadness, and remorse.

I must confess that I have been holding onto a glimmer of hope, hoping that somehow, someway, we could find a path back to each other. I understand that this hope may be unwelcome or burdensome to you, and for that, I am sorry. It was unfair of me to put such pressure on you or to try to persuade you against your own wishes.

I also want to acknowledge the impact of my anxiety, overthinking, and anxious attachment on our relationship. I now see how my constant worrying and need for reassurance may have suffocated our connection and led to feelings of overwhelm and suffocation. I deeply regret the toll it took on you and the strain it placed on our relationship. Please know that I am making concerted efforts to address these patterns and develop healthier ways of coping.

I want to sincerely apologize for not fully comprehending the impact of my actions and behaviour on your emotional well-being. My anxious attachment style often clouded my judgment and made it difficult for me to see beyond my own insecurities. I deeply regret any pain or discomfort I may have caused you, and I am committed to learning from these mistakes and growing into a more secure and emotionally balanced individual.

I understand that my apologies may not alleviate the hurt or undo the damage that has been done. Nevertheless, I want you to know that I genuinely value the time we spent together, the memories we created, and the love we shared. It is with utmost sincerity that I express my remorse and my sincere desire to make amends.

I am committed to my own personal growth and self-improvement, not just for the sake of any future relationship but also for my own well-being. I am actively working on understanding myself better, addressing my anxiety and overthinking tendencies, and fostering healthier relationships in the future. My intention is to become a better person, one who is more self-aware, emotionally available, and capable of building a strong foundation of trust and security.

I understand that you may need time and space to heal and move forward in your own life. I respect and accept any decision you make, as it is your right to prioritize your own happiness and well-being.
